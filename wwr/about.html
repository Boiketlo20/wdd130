History

The roots of river rafting trace back centuries, with indigenous tribes mastering the art of navigating waterways long before it became a recreational sport. Among these early river explorers, the Mandan Tribe, native to the Upper Missouri River, played a significant role in using the river as a lifeline for trade, travel, and survival.

Known for their advanced boat-building techniques, the Mandan constructed sturdy bull boats—round, lightweight vessels made from buffalo hides stretched over a wooden frame. These boats allowed them to navigate the Missouri’s winding currents with ease, facilitating trade with other tribes and early explorers. Today, modern river rafting carries on the spirit of adventure and connection to nature that the Mandan and other indigenous groups established centuries ago.
